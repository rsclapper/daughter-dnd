## NPC Interactions

### Mirabel (If Visited)

**Location**: Archive reading room, surrounded by books

**Read**:
> *Mirabel looks up from a pile of ancient texts. She looks exhausted.*
>
> **Mirabel**: *"Ah. I hoped you'd come. I wanted to... apologize if I seemed pushy in the Council. But you need to understand the stakes."*

**If party returned Codices to seal** (or plans to):
- She's deeply grateful
- Provides detailed map to Fractured Seal
- Warns about Guardian (ancient protector)
- Offers research assistance

**If party chose vault/keeping them**:
- She's worried but respectful of choice
- Warns that seals are weakening (weeks, not months)
- Offers to help if they change minds
- Mentions third Codex (Varn connection)

**Information She Provides** (if asked):
- History of the Nine Seals (Great Binding, 1,200 years ago)
- The Entity (ancient fire being, nearly god-like)
- The Fractured Seal location (15 miles NE, in ravine)
- Varn's grandfather stole third Codex in 1975 (50 years ago)
- Time pressure (seal failing faster than expected)

**Gift** (if party is going to Fractured Seal):
- **Seal Fragment Pendant**: Grants advantage on saves against fire damage (1/day)

---

### Professor Maellin (If Visited)

**Location**: Cramped study, filled with notes on the Codices

**Read**:
> *The professor's study is chaos. Books everywhere, notes pinned to walls, half-eaten meals forgotten on tables. Maellin himself looks terrible—thinner, paler, shaking hands.*
>
> **Maellin**: *"Come in, come in. Forgive the mess. I've been... researching."*

**Condition**: Clearly dying from Codex exposure
- **Symptoms**: Coughing, trembling, can't focus
- **DC 12 Medicine**: Wasting sickness, magical corruption
- **Cure**: Stop researching Codices + Greater Restoration

**What He Knows**:
- Varn funded his research (he didn't know why at first)
- Varn is obsessed with the Fire Entity
- Varn has cult connections (saw cult members in warehouse)
- Varn's goal: Free the Entity, become its vessel
- **WARNING**: "Varn will come for you. He needs those Codices."

**If party asks about cure**:
- He admits he's dying
- Doesn't want to stop research (obsessed)
- Can be convinced with DC 15 Persuasion
- If cured: Becomes grateful ally, provides assistance

**Gift** (if he trusts party):
- **Notes on the Codices**: Advantage on Intelligence checks related to Codices

---

### The Bard (If Visited)

**Location**: Wayfarer's Rest common room, tuning lute

**Read**:
> *The Bard sits in a corner booth, tuning his lute. He grins when he sees you.*
>
> **Bard**: *"The heroes return! Sit, sit. I've been digging into something interesting."*

**Information He Provides**:
- Researched the Fractured Seal (found old maps)
- It's in a ravine, 15 miles northeast
- Ancient ruins, predates Embercross by centuries
- Guarded by something (legends say "stone sentinel")
- Three chambers: Entrance Hall, Hall of Archmages, Seal Chamber

**Offer**:
- "If you're going there, I'm coming. This is history in the making."
- Can accompany party (support NPC, heals, buffs, lore)

**Warning** (if he likes party):
- "Watch your backs tonight. I've heard rumors—assassins in the city asking about you."
- **DC 12 Insight**: He's genuinely concerned

## Research Options (Library or Archives)

**Arcana Check** (about Codices):
- **DC 12**: Flamebound Codices are extremely rare, pre-date modern magic
- **DC 15**: They're tied to binding rituals, act as anchors
- **DC 18**: Nine were created to imprison something (Fire Entity)

**History Check** (about the Fractured Seal):
- **DC 10**: It's northeast of Embercross, old ruins
- **DC 14**: Site of ancient magical working, very old
- **DC 18**: Twelve archmages performed the Great Binding there

**Investigation Check** (about Varn):
- **DC 13**: Varn family has history with Codices
- **DC 16**: Varn's grandfather vanished 50 years ago with an artifact
- **DC 19**: Varn finances cult activities, dangerous connections

---

## Investigation Clues (If Party Looks for Threats)

**If party actively investigates city for danger**:

**Perception Check** (in streets):
- **DC 14**: You notice someone watching you from a rooftop (disappears quickly)
- **DC 17**: Two rough-looking individuals follow you for a block, then duck into an alley

**Investigation Check** (at inn):
- **DC 12**: You notice the window to your room could be easily climbed to
- **DC 15**: Fresh scratches on the window latch (someone tested it recently)
- **DC 18**: A roof tile on the building opposite has been recently disturbed (sniper position)

**Insight Check** (talking to inn staff):
- **DC 13**: The innkeeper seems nervous, mentions "strangers asking about you"
- **DC 16**: A maid says someone paid her to describe your rooms' layout

**Stealth Check** (following suspicious person):
- **DC 15**: You tail someone to a warehouse district
- **Success**: See them meet with hooded figures, discussing "tonight's job"
- **Critical Success**: Overhear: "Midnight. Two Codices. Kill if necessary."

## Defensive Preparations (If Party Prepares)

**If party sets defenses at inn**:

**Options**:
1. **Alarm Spell**: Covers door/windows (if they have it)
2. **Glyph of Warding**: If they have spell slots and components
3. **Physical Barricades**: Stack furniture (takes 10 min)
4. **Set Watch**: Rotating guard duty (prevents surprise)
5. **Trap Windows**: DC 12 DEX to rig makeshift alarm (string + bells)
6. **Request Guard**: Ask Flameguard for protection (only if allied)

**DM Note**: Good preparation can negate surprise round in assassination attempt!

## Time Management

**Track time spent**:
- Each major activity: 10-15 minutes real-time
- Quick shopping: 5 minutes
- Multiple NPCs: Split party (parallel scenes)

**At 4:55 PM real-time**:
**Read**:
> *The sun is setting over Embercross. The city is preparing for dinner and evening festivities. You've had a productive afternoon, but night is approaching.*
>
> *Let's take a 5-minute break. When we return, we'll jump to 11:30 PM in-game—just before midnight.*

## Transition to Break

**Tell Players**:
"Good work everyone. Take 5 minutes to stretch, grab snacks, use the bathroom. When we come back, things are going to get intense. Make sure you're ready for combat."

**DM Prep During Break**:
- Set up combat map (Inn map)
- Review assassination encounter stats
- Note what defenses party set (if any)
- Roll enemy Stealth if party set watch
- Prepare initiative tracker

## DM Notes

**Energy Level**: MODERATE - Calm, exploratory
**Pace**: PLAYER-DRIVEN - Let them choose activities
**Music**: Peaceful exploration, light tension

**Encourage Preparation**:
- Drop hints about danger if they're paying attention
- Reward investigation with clues
- Allow creative defensive measures
- Don't force them—some parties won't prepare

**Common Questions**:
- Q: "Can we skip to the Fractured Seal now?"
  - A: "Sure, but there's an event planned for tonight. Do you want to skip it?" (Assassination attempt)

- Q: "Can we confront Varn?"

- Q: "Can we hire guards?"
  - A: "Yes! Costs 5 gp per guard per night. 1d4 available. AC 13, HP 11, CR 1/8."

**Time Check**: Should end by 5:00 PM, then 5-minute break
