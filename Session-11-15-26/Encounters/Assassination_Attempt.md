# Midnight Strike - Combat Encounter

## Overview
**Type**: Combat / Investigation
**Duration**: 30-60 minutes (combat) + 15-30 minutes (investigation)
**Difficulty**: Deadly (CR 9-10 encounter for level 7-8 party)
**Location**: Wayfarer's Rest Inn
**Objective**: Survive the assassination attempt and uncover Varn's involvement

---

## Trigger & Timing

### When This Happens
**Evening of the Council Meeting** (8-10 hours after Council adjourns)

**Trigger Options**:
1. **Party Sleeps**: Attack comes at midnight (classic)
2. **Party Is at Dinner**: Attack during meal in common room
3. **Party Returns to Inn**: Ambush as they enter
4. **Party Is Preparing**: Strike while packing/planning

**DM Choice**: Pick timing based on party actions and dramatic tension

### Pre-Attack Warning Signs (Optional)

**Subtle Clues** (DC 14-16 Perception):
- Inn staff acting nervous (bribed by assassins)
- Feeling of being watched
- Unusual quiet in normally bustling inn
- Shadow moving on rooftop across street
- Locked window is now slightly open

**The Bard's Warning** (If Present):
- "Something feels off. Maybe we should set watches tonight?"
- Not specific, just general unease

**Professor Maellin's Warning** (If Befriended):
- Sends urgent note: "Varn knows where you are. MOVE. NOW."
- May arrive personally to warn them

## The Assassination Team

### Enemy Composition (Adjust for Party Level)

**Full Team** (Deadly for Level 7-8):
- **The Midnight Blade** (Leader) - CR 7
- **Raven** (Sniper) - CR 5
- **Wraith** (Infiltrator) - CR 4
- **Crusher** (Bruiser) - CR 5
- **2x Flamebrand Zealots** - CR 2 each

**Total XP**: ~4,200 XP (Deadly for 4x Level 7 characters)

**Scaled for Level 5-6** (Remove Crusher and 1 Zealot):
- Midnight Blade + Raven + Wraith + 1 Zealot = ~2,400 XP

**Scaled for Level 9-10** (Add reinforcements):
- Full team + Cult Caster (CR 5) + 2 more Zealots = ~6,000 XP

### Enemy Stat Blocks (Quick Reference)

**THE MIDNIGHT BLADE** (Leader)
- AC 17, HP 95
- Shortsword +1: +10 to hit, 1d6+6 + 4d6 sneak attack
- Shadow Dagger (teleport to blade 30 ft)
- **Tactics**: Stealth, sneak attack, hit-and-run
- **Retreat**: 50% HP or 2+ allies down

**RAVEN** (Sniper)
- AC 16, HP 65
- Longbow +1: +8 to hit, 1d8+5 + poison (DC 14 CON or 2d8 poison)
- **Location**: Rooftop across street (180 ft away)
- **Tactics**: Snipe priority targets, retreat if approached

**WRAITH** (Infiltrator)
- AC 15, HP 45
- Shortsword: +7 to hit, 1d6+4 + 2d6 sneak attack
- **Tools**: Smoke bombs, climbing gear
- **Tactics**: Flank, create chaos, steal Codices

**CRUSHER** (Bruiser)
- AC 16, HP 85
- Greataxe: +7 to hit, 1d12+4 slashing
- **Feat**: Sentinel (locks down enemies)
- **Tactics**: Guard exits, prevent escape, tank

**FLAMEBRAND ZEALOTS** (x2)
- AC 13, HP 30 each
- Scimitar: +5 to hit, 1d6+3 + 1d6 fire
- **Fanatical**: Won't retreat, fight to death
- **Tactics**: Reckless charges, create distractions

## The Battlefield: Wayfarer's Rest Inn

### Inn Layout (3 Floors)

**Ground Floor** (Common Room):
- Bar along north wall
- 8 tables with chairs (cover, improvised weapons)
- Fireplace on east wall (environmental hazard)
- Main entrance on south wall
- Kitchen door on west wall
- Stairs to upper floors (center)

**Second Floor** (Guest Rooms):
- Central hallway
- 6 guest rooms (3 per side)
- Party rooms: 2-3 adjacent rooms
- Windows overlooking street
- Balcony at end of hall

**Third Floor** (Premium Suites):
- 2 large suites
- Better furnishings
- Larger windows
- Roof access via hatch

**Exterior**:
- Street (20 ft wide)
- Opposing building with rooftop (Raven's position)
- Back alley (escape route)
- Stable (horses, more chaos potential)

### Terrain Features

**Cover**:
- Tables/chairs (half cover, AC +2)
- Bar (three-quarters cover, AC +5)
- Walls (full cover)
- Furniture (can be overturned for cover)

**Hazards**:
- Fireplace (push enemy in: DC 14 STR, 2d6 fire damage)
- Windows (break through: DC 12 STR, glass shards)
- Stairs (difficult terrain, knock down: DC 13 DEX save)
- Oil lamps (throw: improvised weapon, 1d4 + fire spread)

**Vertical Elements**:
- Stairs between floors
- Balcony (jump down: 10 ft, 1d6 damage)
- Rooftop (climb/jump between buildings)

## Battle Flow

### Phase 1: Infiltration (If Party Is Sleeping)

**Round -1** (Before combat initiative):
- Wraith picks party room locks (DC 18 Perception to hear)
- Midnight Blade enters adjacent room (window)
- Raven positions on rooftop across street
- Crusher waits in alley below
- Zealots wait in common room

**Detection**:
- **Alert Party Member**: DC 14 Perception while on watch
- **Light Sleeper**: DC 16 Perception while sleeping
- **Alarm Ward**: Automatically triggers
- **Codex Reaction**: Glow when hostile intent nearby (automatic)

**If Undetected**:
- Midnight Blade gets Assassinate feature (auto-crit on surprised)
- Wraith enters second room simultaneously
- Surprise round for assassins

**If Detected**:
- Party gets to act in initiative order
- No surprise round
- Alarm raises inn (civilians flee, chaos)

### Phase 2: Initial Combat (Rounds 1-3)

**Assassin Tactics**:
1. **Midnight Blade**: Targets Codex carrier, sneak attack, grab and run
2. **Wraith**: Flanks, uses smoke bomb for obscurement
3. **Raven**: Snipes casters from rooftop
4. **Crusher**: Blocks stairs if party tries to flee
5. **Zealots**: Charge recklessly, create chaos

**Environmental Chaos**:
- Inn guests flee screaming (movement obstacles)
- Fire spreads from combat (increasing hazard)
- Furniture breaks (difficult terrain)
- Windows shatter (glass on floor)

**Dynamic Elements**:
- Civilian caught in crossfire? (Save them or focus on assassins?)
- Inn staff try to help? (Distraction or resource?)
- Flameguard patrol hears commotion? (Reinforcements in 5 rounds)

### Phase 3: Escalation (Rounds 4-6)

**Midnight Blade's Decision Point** (Round 4):
- **If Codices Acquired**: Fighting retreat with prize
- **If Party Winning**: Tactical withdrawal
- **If Close Fight**: Press attack
- **If Party Crushing**: Smoke bomb and flee

**Wraith's Secondary Objective**:
- If Codex grab fails, steal valuables (distraction)
- Uses smoke bombs to create confusion
- Attempts parkour escape via windows

**Raven's Problem**:
- If party sends someone after them (climbing/flying)
- Decision: Keep sniping or retreat across rooftops
- Will retreat if reduced to 50% HP

**Crusher's Stand**:
- Fights to death or until Midnight Blade calls retreat
- Uses Sentinel to lock down pursuers
- Allows team to escape

**Zealots' Fanaticism**:
- Fight to death shouting "For the Eternal Flame!"
- Cult tattoos visible when shirts burn off
- One might survive long enough to be interrogated

### Phase 4: Resolution (Rounds 7+)

**Victory Conditions**:
- **Party**: Defeat/capture assassins, protect Codices
- **Assassins**: Acquire Codices and escape OR escape alive

**Likely Outcomes**:
1. **Party Wins Decisively**: Most/all assassins killed or captured
2. **Party Wins Costly**: Assassins defeated but party injured, inn damaged
3. **Stalemate**: Assassins retreat without Codices, party survives
4. **Partial Success**: Assassins steal one Codex, escape with it
5. **Disaster**: Both Codices stolen (unlikely unless party is underleveled)

**Flameguard Arrival** (Round 8-10):
- 6 Flameguard soldiers arrive (CR 3 total)
- Led by captain
- Secure scene, arrest surviving assassins
- Thrane arrives shortly after

## Special Combat Events (Optional)

### Roll d8 Each Round for Chaos

**1. Civilian in Danger**
- Inn guest caught in combat zone
- Screaming, running toward danger
- Party must save them OR let them get hurt
- Moral choice under pressure

**2. Fire Spreads**
- Combat has ignited furnishings
- 10x10 area becomes difficult terrain + 1d6 fire/round
- Spreads 5 ft each round
- Someone must stop it or inn burns

**3. Codex Reacts**
- Pulses with energy
- Entity whispers: "Yesss... more blood..."
- All combatants DC 13 WIS save or frightened 1 round
- Reveals Entity is aware and enjoying this

**4. Assassin Reinforcement**
- Midnight Blade calls backup (1 more Zealot)
- Enters from unexpected direction
- Or Varn's mage provides magical support

**5. Structural Damage**
- Floor/ceiling weakens from combat
- 10x10 area becomes difficult terrain
- DC 14 DEX save or fall through to floor below (1d6 damage)

**6. Innocent Bystander Helps**
- Inn staff throws improvised weapon at assassin
- Distracts enemy (grants advantage to one PC)
- Shows community supporting heroes

**7. Codex Falls**
- Combat knocks Codex from carrier's possession
- Lands 15 ft away
- Both sides scramble for it
- Initiative challenge

**8. No Event**
- Combat proceeds normally
- Good round for tactics

## After the Battle

### Immediate Aftermath

**Scene Description**:
> *The last assassin falls. Silence descends, broken only by crackling flames and distant city watch whistles. The inn's common room is destroyed - overturned tables, shattered glass, scorch marks on walls. [Civilian casualties if any]. The Codices [still in your possession / recovered / stolen]. And in the distance, the sound of Flameguard boots approaching.*

**Party Condition**:
- HP damage from combat
- Spell slots expended
- Equipment damage possible
- Civilians' reactions (grateful, terrified, accusing)

**Inn Damage**:
- Common room destroyed
- Some rooms damaged
- Fire damage (if not controlled)
- Innkeeper wants compensation (or grateful if saved)

**Civilian Reaction**:
- If party protected them: "You saved us!"
- If casualties: "Why did this happen here?!"
- If efficient/minimal damage: Impressed by skill
- If messy/destructive: Frightened of power

### Flameguard Response (5-10 Minutes After)

**Arrival**:
- 6 soldiers + Captain
- Secure the scene
- Arrest surviving assassins
- Take witness statements
- Councilor Thrane arrives within 30 minutes

**Thrane's Reaction**:
- "What happened here?!"
- Listens to party's account
- Examines bodies and evidence
- **If Vault Chosen**: "This proves my point about needing protection."
- **If Other Choice**: "Perhaps you should reconsider the vault?"

**Investigation** (Flameguard conducts):
- Bodies searched
- Witnesses interviewed
- Evidence collected
- Report filed

### Interrogation (If Assassin Captured Alive)

**Likely Captive**: Wraith or injured Midnight Blade or Zealot

**Zealot** (Fanatic - Hard to Break):
- "The Eternal Flame will rise!"
- Refuses to betray Varn
- Can be broken with DC 18 Intimidation/Persuasion
- Reveals: "The Prophet hired us to reclaim the keys"
- "The Prophet" = Varn (doesn't use name)

**Wraith** (Professional - Negotiable):
- "I want a deal. Immunity and I talk."
- Reveals contract details willingly if guaranteed safety
- Names "wealthy noble, red and gold colors" (Varn)
- Knows drop point: Crimson Warehouse
- Doesn't know bigger picture (just hired muscle)

**Midnight Blade** (If Captured - Respectful):
- Calm, professional even in defeat
- Answers tactical questions honestly
- Confirms anonymous contract but suspected Varn
- "The contract is void. I failed. You fought well."
- Won't betray other clients (professional code)
- Might warn: "He'll send others. Better ones."

## Evidence & Investigation

### On Assassins' Bodies

**The Midnight Blade's Equipment**:
- **Contract**: Expensive paper with Varn family watermark (DC 18 Investigation)
- **Payment**: 500 gold in coins (traceable mint marks)
- **Map**: Inn layout with party rooms marked
- **Orders**: "Acquire artifacts. Minimize casualties."

**Raven's Equipment**:
- **Poison Vials**: 3 doses (rare, expensive, 25 gp each)
- **Longbow +1**: Masterwork (300 gp value)
- **Spyglass**: For surveillance

**Wraith's Equipment**:
- **Thieves' Tools**: Masterwork (100 gp)
- **Smoke Bombs**: 2 remaining
- **Grappling Hook**: Pre-positioned escape gear

**Crusher's Equipment**:
- **Greataxe**: Quality but unremarkable
- **Letter**: "Guard the exit. Let none escape. -M.B."

**Zealots' Evidence** (CRITICAL):
- **Flamebrand Tattoos**: Phoenix rising from ashes on chest
- **Holy Symbols**: Small flame pendants
- **Prayer Scrolls**: Cult prayers to "Eternal Flame" and "Prophet"
- **Link to Varn**: Scrolls reference "Prophet's Vision" in Varn-like language

### Crime Scene Investigation

**DC 12 Investigation**:
- Professional operation, significant funding
- Planned approach (maps, bribes, reconnaissance)
- Multiple entry points used

**DC 15 Investigation**:
- Paper watermark matches noble house (Varn)
- Poison from specific alchemist in trade district
- Coins minted in Vexler (Varn's home base)

**DC 18 Investigation**:
- Cult symbols connect to Flamebrand (known cult)
- Payment trail could be tracked to source
- Drop point mentioned: Crimson Warehouse (Varn property)

**DC 20 Investigation**:
- Fingerprint/signature analysis on contract
- Links directly to Varn family
- Enough evidence for arrest warrant (maybe)

### The Crimson Warehouse (Optional Follow-Up)

**If Party Investigates**:
- Dock district warehouse
- Owned by Varn's shipping company (public record)
- Currently "empty" (cover)
- Actually: Minor cult meeting place

**Stakeout Results**:
- Cult members come and go
- Warehouse has secret basement
- Could raid for more evidence
- Or use to track Varn's operations

## DM Notes

### Pacing the Combat

**Rounds 1-2**: Chaos and surprise
**Rounds 3-4**: Party gains upper hand
**Rounds 5-6**: Assassins attempt retreat OR final stand
**Rounds 7+**: Cleanup and resolution

**Keep It Dynamic**:
- Use environmental events
- Civilian chaos
- Vertical terrain
- Assassin communication (party can hear tactics)

### Adjusting Difficulty On-The-Fly

**If Party Is Dominating**:
- Reinforcements arrive (1-2 more zealots)
- Midnight Blade fights smarter (better tactics)
- Environmental hazards (fire spreads)

**If Party Is Struggling**:
- Flameguard arrives early (Round 5-6)
- NPC ally helps (Bard, Maellin, Mirabel)
- Assassins focus on grab-and-run not kills
- Midnight Blade calls retreat earlier

**If TPK Risk**:
- Assassins achieve objective (steal Codices) and flee
- Don't fight to death
- Creates rescue mission instead of game over

### Making It Memorable

**Cinematic Moments**:
- Midnight Blade and PC face off in doorway
- Raven's arrow narrowly misses PC's head
- Zealot's dying words about "the Prophet"
- Codex glowing during combat
- PC heroically saves civilian

**Emotional Beats**:
- Fear when attack begins
- Determination during fight
- Relief when survived
- Anger at Varn when evidence found

**Player Agency**:
- Let them use environment creatively
- Reward tactical thinking
- Allow creative solutions
- Honor bold plays

### The Investigation Is Important

**Don't Skip It**:
- Finding Varn connection is major plot point
- Evidence gathering is satisfying
- Interrogation provides information and choices
- Sets up future confrontation with Varn

**Let Players Lead**:
- They ask what to search for
- You provide what they'd find
- Skill checks reveal details
- Piece together the puzzle

## Variant Scenarios

### If Codices Are In Vault (Council Choice)

**Assassins Hit Vault Instead**:
- More difficult heist
- Party hears alarm, respond
- Fight in/around Flameguard HQ
- Different terrain, more guards

**Outcome**:
- Proves vault isn't impregnable
- Thrane embarrassed but grateful
- May change party's opinion

### If Party Is Away from Inn

**Ambush On Street**:
- Open combat in city square
- More civilians at risk
- City watch responds faster
- Different terrain (market stalls, fountain)

**Ambush On Road** (Traveling to Fractured Seal):
- Wilderness terrain
- No reinforcements
- Harder to escape
- More isolated, scary

### If Party Set Elaborate Defenses

**Clever Party**:
- Alarm spells: Assassins trigger them
- Traps: Some assassins caught
- Decoys: Midnight Blade sees through (DC 16 Investigation)
- Guards: Hired NPCs help in combat

**Reward Preparation**:
- Give advantage on initiative
- Some assassins taken out before combat
- Reduce enemy numbers
